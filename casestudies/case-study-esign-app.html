Built an in-house digital document signing tool to replace DocuSign and reduce cost and dependency.

Redesigned the document signing experience to keep the same usability while moving to an internal system.

Role UI/UX Designer & UI Developer
Duration Multi-month Project
Output Web-based document signing tool (UI + Dev)
[Main eSign Tool Hero Image]

Problem Statement

The company was using DocuSign for digital document signing, handling hundreds of documents every month. This created ongoing cost, dependency on a third-party tool, and limited control over customization. The goal was to build an internal signing tool with the same core functionality using an existing PDFTron subscription already used by other teams.

Why this mattered

Replacing DocuSign was not just a technical task. It was a usability risk. This problem mattered for both users and the business because:

  • Cost impact: DocuSign was used frequently, which made licensing expensive at scale.
  • Operational dependency: Any limitation or change in DocuSign directly affected internal workflows.
  • Fragmented tooling: Other teams already used PDFTron, but document signing lived outside the ecosystem.
  • User risk: Employees and clients were used to DocuSign. Any downgrade in experience could cause confusion or errors.
  • Business risk: Poor signing flow could lead to unsigned documents, delays, or legal issues.

Hypothesis

What we concluded

Users did not care which tool was used. They cared about speed, clarity, and trust. The new tool had to feel familiar and predictable. Missing or unclear signing steps would create errors and delays.


What we did

We kept the signing flow very close to DocuSign, focused on clarity over new features, and used PDFTron’s capabilities to recreate essential signing actions without overbuilding.

Design Process

Explore

  • Studied the existing DocuSign flow step by step.
  • Mapped required actions: view document, place signature, review, submit.
  • Explored how much of this PDFTron supported out of the box.

Iterate

  • Designed simple layouts focused on the document itself.
  • Tested different placements for signing actions.
  • Reduced distractions around the PDF viewer.

Rejected: Adding extra features beyond DocuSign or custom layouts that looked “new” but risked confusion. This was a replacement tool, not a redesign for novelty.

Implement

  • Built the UI directly while designing to stay close to technical limits.
  • Matched behavior closely to DocuSign: Clear signing indicators, Step-by-step flow, and Strong visual feedback after actions.

Constraints

  • Existing PDFTron API limits.
  • No formal user research.
  • Internal deadlines.
  • Tool used by both employees and external clients.

Final Solution

Results in the experience:

  • Replaced DocuSign with an internal signing tool.
  • Preserved familiar signing patterns to avoid relearning.
  • Integrated tightly with existing internal systems.
  • Used PDFTron to handle PDFs while controlling the UX layer.
  • Fewer steps to complete signing.
  • Clear visual hierarchy around required actions.
  • Reduced chance of missing signatures.
  • Consistent experience for internal users and clients.

Impact & Learnings

Impact

  • Reduced reliance on a third-party tool.
  • Lower operational cost over time.
  • Smoother internal workflow.
  • Users were able to complete signing without training or support.

Learnings

Replacing a known tool is more risky than building something new. Familiarity is a feature.
