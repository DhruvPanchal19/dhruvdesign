Made it faster and easier for users to find support documents.

Redesigned knowledge base search to improve usability, speed, and accuracy

Role UI/UX Designer & UI Developer
Duration 1 Month
Output AI-powered Search Interface

Problem Statement

Users struggled to find or verify information in the support portal’s large knowledge base unless they already knew the exact document name or path. If users couldn’t find what they needed, they had to contact their Strategic Account Manager (SAM) or open a support ticket.

Why this mattered

This was a real problem because:

  • Most users didn’t know the exact document titles or locations.
  • Search was frustrating, slow, and often led to dead ends.
  • Users wasted time, or abandoned self-service, increasing pressure on SAMs and support teams.
  • Every ticket raised for basic information created extra workload and slowed down resolution for everyone.

Hypothesis

What we concluded

  • The main problem was strict search that relied on exact matches.
  • Users needed a search that understood intent, not just keywords.
  • The UI made it hard to skim or filter results.

What we did

  • Integrated AI-powered, semantic search to deliver more human results.
  • Redesigned the search UI for faster scanning and easier filtering.
  • Result: Users could find what they needed in under 30 seconds instead of 5 minutes or more.

Process

Explore

Looked at options: keyword-only search, advanced filters, and AI/semantic search. Explored different layouts: list, card, and grouped results.

Iterate

Tested card and list views. Card view took up too much space, made skimming slower. List view with highlights worked best. Tried adding too many filters—users got overwhelmed. Kept only the most useful ones.

Implement

Went with a clean list layout, with key info and highlights. Added a “Did you mean” feature for mistyped queries and integrated semantic AI search to handle broad, natural questions. Worked under a tight 1-month timeline and with legacy data structures.

Screenshots & Notes

Old Search Page (Context): Previously, filters were cluttered, there were no suggested results, and it relied on strict keyword matching. Users had to know exact titles or paths.


New Search Page: Clean list view, "Did you mean" suggestions, humanized summaries, and highlighted keywords. Users can search in plain language and scan results quickly.

AI-Powered Results: User asks a question, system returns the most relevant answers, not just keyword matches. Demonstrates improved matching and usability.

Final Solution

  • Switched to a smarter, AI-powered search that understands user questions, not just keywords.
  • Reduced clutter and made the search results easier to scan and filter.
  • Added suggestions and highlights to guide users.
  • Users now find what they need in less than 30 seconds, down from 5 minutes or more.
  • Fewer support tickets for basic document requests.

Impact & Learnings

  • Users got answers faster and with less frustration.
  • Fewer tickets raised for “can’t find document” issues.
  • SAMs had more time for complex support, not basic queries.

Design learning: Cutting extra filters and focusing on a clear result list made a bigger difference than adding more features.

What I’d improve next time: Would run more quick usability tests early to fine-tune search suggestions before launch.
